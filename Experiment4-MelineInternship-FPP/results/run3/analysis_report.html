Variable Impact on Performance

Statistical Method: Variance decomposition analysis

The impact score quantifies the proportion of total variance in the performance metric that is explained by each variable. For each variable, the metric values are grouped by the variable's levels, and the between-group variance is computed. The impact score is calculated as:

Impact = σ²_between / σ²_total

where σ²_between is the variance of group means (weighted by group size) and σ²_total is the overall variance of the metric. This measure is analogous to the R² coefficient in regression or the eta-squared (η²) effect size in ANOVA. Values range from 0 (no effect) to 1 (variable fully determines the metric).
